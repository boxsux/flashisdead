Flash is dead

But it wasn't always that way.

Flash tied the web together. It was the plugin to do all sorts of things, ranging from interactivity to plain images. It had a time and a place. However, we've come to a time where it's simply not needed anymore, because all of those features that Flash offered are available now in HTML5.

As of December 31, 2020, Flash has reached its EOL.

In addition, Adobe started blocking Flash on January 12, 2021.

Can I do anything about it as a user?

Short answer: no. Long answer... it depends.

If you know someone who worked on a game for Flash and you want it ported to HTML5, contact them. This is most common for Newgrounds content, and even some stuff on Cool Math Games.

What if I'm a web developer?

You're stuck with newer technology. You seriously don't need Flash to render things on the modern web nowadays. Browsers don't support Flash anymore, and even if you download special tools that add compatibility for Flash, you still have to use third-party programs to use Flash things.
